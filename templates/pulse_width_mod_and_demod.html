Pulse Width Modulation and Demodulation

This tool simulates Pulse Width Modulation (PWM). PWM varies the pulse width of a carrier in proportion to a modulating signal and is widely used in motor control, power converters, and digital-to-analog emulation. Configure the sampling frequency, PWM carrier frequency, simulation duration, and a random seed to generate reproducible waveforms.

Sampling Frequency (fs)
Rate at which signals are sampled for the simulation. Higher fs gives finer resolution. Typical: 1 kHz‚Äì50 kHz.
PWM Carrier Frequency (f_pwm)
Switching frequency of the PWM signal (carrier). Higher values reduce harmonic energy at low frequencies but increase switching losses in hardware. Typical: 20 Hz‚Äì20 kHz for demo; up to 100s of kHz in power electronics.
Simulation Duration
Length of simulated time (seconds). Choose long enough to observe several PWM cycles. Typical: 0.01‚Äì0.5 s.
Random Seed
Provides deterministic randomness for any noise or jitter added. Use the same seed for repeatable runs.
